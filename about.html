Understanding the 2018 Farm Bill and what makes our products legal

The 2018 Farm Bill

The Agriculture Improvement Act of 2018 (Farm Bill) federally legalized hemp and hemp-derived products containing less than 0.3% Delta-9 THC on a dry weight basis. This landmark legislation removed hemp from the Controlled Substances Act, making it legal to grow, process, and sell hemp products across the United States.

What is THCA?

THCA (tetrahydrocannabinolic acid) is the non-psychoactive precursor to THC found in raw cannabis. It only converts to THC when heated (decarboxylation). Our THCA flower is derived from hemp plants that contain less than 0.3% Delta-9 THC, making them fully compliant with federal law.

Lab Testing & Compliance

Every batch of our products is third-party lab tested to verify cannabinoid content, terpene profiles, and the absence of contaminants. We maintain full compliance with federal regulations and provide Certificates of Analysis (COA) for complete transparency.

Interstate Commerce

Because our products are hemp-derived and Farm Bill compliant, they can be legally shipped across state lines. We ship to all 50 states (excluding restrictions where state law prohibits hemp-derived THCA products). We stay updated on state regulations to ensure compliance.
